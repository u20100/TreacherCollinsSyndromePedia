Genetic Aspect

  • The TCOF1 gene is located on chromosome 5q32
  • TCOF1 encodes for Treacle, a serine/alanine-rich, nucleolar phosphoprotein.
  • Treacle is active throughout early embryonic development in structures that become bones and other tissues of the face.
  • The mutant gene in TCS TCOF1 was discovered using genetic, physical, and transcript mapping approaches.
  • 130 other mutations have been discovered which include: insertion, splicing, and non-sense mutations. Most deletions occur between 1 and 40 nucleotides in length.
  • 40% of TCS cases show a prior family history, 60% of instances don't appear to have a prior family history and are likely to be the product of a de novo mutation.

Timeline of Diagnosis of Treacher-Collins Syndrome

Prenatal Stage

Diagnosis Means

2D Radiography

More common method of detection.

Accompanied by genetic testing.

2D sonography show less details of the fetal features. The mandible angle and inferior facial angle indicated hypoplastic angle.

3D/4D Radiography

3D/4D imaging show fetal features that aid in the differential diagnosis of TCS. Unlike 2D imaging, 3D/4D can show micrognathia,palpebral downslanting, macrostomia, and microtia
