Orbital Soft Tissue Deformities

    Orbitl Deformities

  • Lateral orbital rim hypoplasia, orbital volume deficiency, lateral lower eyelid colobomas, antimongoloid slant, lateral canthal dystopia, atresia of the lacrimal system, and medial eyelash deficiency

    • Ophthalmological manifestations

  • Vision loss due to amblyopia, strabismus, coloboma, and refractive errors

    • Treatment

  • Treatment is achieved by Tessier's zygoma plasty to increase anterior and lateral projection of the malar eminences .
  • Follow-up after the procedure ranges from 4 to 15 months and usually no complications are associated.
  • Treatment is targeting interventions aimed to preserve vision, protect the ocular surface, and reconstruct the bony and soft tissue dysmorphologies to produce a functional and cosmetic periorbital outcome .
