Breathing

Upper airway obstruction critical and potentially fatal . Recognition of micrognathia and airway obstruction are of primary concern in newborns with TCS.

    Narrowing of the airway is due to:

  • TMJ anomalies
  • cleft palate
  • glossoptosis
  • micrognathia and retrognathia

(Source: Craniofacial Microsomia and Treacher Collins Syndrome )

In severe cases, choanal atresia is present. Choanal atresia is the blockage of the inferior openings of the nasal cavity or choanae. These defects combine and can potentially result in acute respiratory distress.
